# Weather Trading Hypotheses — Autonomous Test Catalog

## Intro

This catalog is the build queue for an autonomous Kalshi weather-trading agent run by **one operator targeting ~$50k/yr**, where the binding constraint is **market capacity, not capital**. Every hypothesis here is scoped to a single, narrow edge: **superior calibration/processing of free public data versus the crude marginal price-setter, in markets firms ignore.** It is explicitly **not** a forecasting bet (you lose to ECMWF/NWS/NBM, which the price already reflects) and **not** a latency race (on-venue feed-readers delete public-feed EV — that is the firms' lane).

The alpha is a product of three terms that must all be non-zero: **(retail/crude-bot error) × (firm disinterest) × (our processing edge)**. Remove any one and EV = 0. Concretely, our counterparty is the "vibe-coded bot" that anchors a single public **point** forecast through a fixed-width curve (the repo's own `weather.py` is exactly this: Open-Meteo `temperature_2m_max` → fixed 2.5F logistic, ~zero edge). We beat it by representing **state-dependent uncertainty** (ensemble spread), **exact settlement replication** (CLI whole-F C-round, LST window, named station, intraday running-max floor), and **calibrated tail/longshot processing** — but only where a measured **price-vs-feed responsiveness screen** confirms the book is genuinely stale. Edge is treated as **decaying rent, not property**: rotate in as a market lags, rotate out as it sharpens.

Two cross-cutting disciplines bind the whole catalog:
- **Settlement basis is the cardinal sin.** Trade only the exact settling statistic (NWS CLI whole-degree-F, ASOS 2-min-avg internal running max, LST climate-day, the specific named station — e.g. CHI = **KMDW Midway**, not KORD O'Hare; NYC = **KNYC** Central Park). Proxies (gridded cells, own hardware, nearby sensors) are disqualifying. **Latent repo bug:** `weather.py` maps CHI→KORD; this is a live settlement-basis defect and must be fixed before any CHI trade.
- **Calibrate before you trade.** Most of these read off a calibrated PMF; if your own PMF is the thing that is miscalibrated, you systematically sell the true mode or buy fairly-priced tails. Rank-histogram/PIT flatness is a precondition, not a nicety.

A note on testability that recurs below: **per-member ensemble history is not reconstructable** (must forward-log), **NBM/MOS text has no public archive** (1–2 day retention), **AIFS ensemble archive on Open-Meteo only extends back ~3 months**, **Kalshi does not archive orderbook depth** (only 1-min candlestick OHLC), and `outputs/snapshots.jsonl` currently has ~5 rows. Several high-ceiling hypotheses are therefore **forward-data-collection projects**, not backtestable this week. This is flagged per item and in the Caveats.

## Category A — Ensemble Calibration (state-dependent width vs the fixed-2.5F anchor)

This is the load-bearing family. H1 is the foundation; H3 and H4 are overlays on it; H5 is its sharpest relative-value expression; H6 stacks a behavioral edge on top.

### H1. EMOS spread-scaled bucket PMF replaces the fixed 2.5F logistic
*(merge: H2 "Spread-inflation correction" folded in as the degenerate fallback — see note; H17/H22 "NBP percentile fan" and H29 "AIFS-ENS EMOS" are the station-product and AI-model instantiations of the same core and cross-reference here.)*
- **Thesis:** The crude setter maps ONE point forecast through a fixed-width 2.5F logistic, so its bucket PMF has constant width regardless of regime — overconfident in the center on volatile days (sells tails too cheap), underconfident on calm days. True high-uncertainty swings ~1.5F (calm ridge) to 6–10F (frontal). We process the same free public ensemble into a calibrated per-station width.
- **Signal:** Pool per-member `temperature_2m_max` from Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` (ifs_025_ensemble 51 + gfs_ensemble_025 31 + aifs_025_ensemble 51). Fit 2-param EMOS/NGR per station: `high ~ N(a + b·ens_mean, c + d·ens_var)`, minimizing CRPS on a rolling trailing window of (ensemble, realized CLI high) pairs. Read `P(bucket=[floor,cap])` off the calibrated Normal CDF, renormalize the ladder to sum=1. Trade where `|market_mid − fair| > spread + fee`. Abstain on cold start (`fair_prob_yes=None`).
- **Data:** Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` (keyless, daily=`temperature_2m_max`, unit=F); NWS CLI Daily Climate Report (training labels + backtest truth); Kalshi `/events?series_ticker=&with_nested_markets=true` (ladder geometry); Open-Meteo Historical-Forecast API (as_of_date no-lookahead replay).
- **Settlement check:** Final NWS CLI daily high, whole-deg-F, ASOS 2-min-avg 1-min samples, **LST year-round** (DST → climate-day 1:00AM–12:59AM local civil). EMOS bias term **`a` is mandatory** — it maps the 0.25° grid cell to the named settling station. Fit `a,b,c,d` against CLI only, never ERA5/grid. **Whole-F rounding dominates within ~1.5F of a bucket edge:** discretize the calibrated CDF onto the integer-F lattice before integrating over the 2F bucket.
- **Backtest + metric:** No-lookahead rolling EMOS fit on station-days strictly before each event (Historical-Forecast ensemble-as-of + CLI truth). Forward-log live members into snapshots.jsonl (per-member history not reconstructable). Metric: multi-bucket log-loss/Brier + CRPS vs realized CLI bucket, A/B against the fixed-2.5F logistic; require strict improvement **plus** positive simulated EV net of taker fee at snapshot mids. Secondary: PIT/rank-histogram flatness.
- **Capacity:** 8+ HIGH cities × ~6 buckets × daily = dozens of small fills/day; scales by breadth, capped by per-bucket top-of-book depth (`MIN_TOP_LIQUIDITY=25`).
- **Beats whom:** Crude point-anchored bots (fixed-width logistic) and retail; firms ignore the small ladders.
- **Decay risk:** A feed-reading firm/NGR-running firm deletes this in liquid headline cities (NYC/LAX/CHI/MIA) — that is table-stakes calibration. Survives only in neglected small-city ladders and on volatile/frontal days. Center-bucket EV arbs first; surviving rent is in shoulder buckets of thin ladders. EMOS params drift seasonally → re-fit continuously. Detect sharpening via H7, rotate out.
- **Autonomy plan:** Nightly: enumerate open `KXHIGH*`/`KXLOW*` events, map ticker→station; fetch pooled members; update rolling EMOS from stored (ensemble, CLI) pairs; compute renormalized PMF, diff vs ladder mids; emit `fair_prob_yes` per bucket; `strategy.decide` gates on `MIN_EDGE_CENTS` + fee buffer; RiskGate sizes; next morning scrape CLI to append labels and update the realized-edge ledger.
- **Sharpenings (mandatory):** (1) pool a **hierarchical prior across cities** for the b/d slope terms — 4 params per station on a short window is noisy/overfits seasonal regime; (2) fix the CHI→KORD repo bug; (3) discretize onto the actual (uneven) integer-F lattice. Testability=4 (member history must be forward-logged; CLI scraping brittle; past dates lose true member spread). **Necessary-not-sufficient: converts the bot from crude to calibrated, which only earns where a firm isn't already doing the same.**

> **Merged: H2 — Spread-inflation correction for under-dispersed AI/NBM ensembles.** Subsumed by H1: a properly fit NGR `d` variance term already learns the correct conditional variance, making a separate scalar inflation `k` redundant. **Keep only as the degenerate fallback/cross-check** when pricing off RAW members/percentiles without a variance fit (which you shouldn't). The under-dispersion fact (e.g. NBM P90 verifies ~84% not 90%) is the *prior* that justifies H1's variance inflation and is reused wherever a tail mass is read (H6, H25). Do not staff as its own track.

### H3. Multi-model AI-vs-physics disagreement regime gate (overlay on H1)
*(merge: H30 "AI-vs-physics disagreement" and H21 "tri-product mu-disagreement gate" are the AI-model and station-product variants of this same orthogonal signal — see notes.)*
- **Thesis:** When AI-ensemble mean (AIFS/AI-GEFS) and physics-ensemble mean (IFS/GFS) disagree by more than within-ensemble spread, genuine **structural** state uncertainty exceeds any single ensemble's spread (the herd's single anchor cannot see it). Tight agreement → center bucket safer than a fixed-width price implies.
- **Signal:** `mu_ai = mean(aifs+aigefs daily max)`, `mu_phys = mean(ifs+gfs daily max)`. Disagreement `D = |mu_ai − mu_phys|`; within-ensemble pooled std `S`. If `D > α·S` → inflate width by f(D) or abstain; if `D < β·S` and low spread → lean into modal bucket where market underprices it. Fit α,β on held-out CRPS.
- **Data:** Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` single call `models=aifs_025_ensemble,ifs_025_ensemble,gfs_ensemble_025,aigefs_025`; NWS CLI; Kalshi ladder.
- **Settlement check:** D is scale-relative so robust to shared grid bias, but the final bucket fair still needs H1's station location term. LST re-windowing of daily max; CLI whole-F is truth.
- **Backtest + metric:** Bin realized `|CLI − mu|` by D and check **monotonicity / that D adds conditional-variance skill beyond within-ensemble spread S** — this is the kill test, run it first. Then A/B D-gated vs non-gated EMOS width on log-loss/CRPS, no-lookahead. Success = improved OOS calibration AND higher EV by concentrating size on agreement days.
- **Capacity:** Size/selectivity overlay across all temp ladders; near-zero standalone capacity.
- **Beats whom:** Single-model crude bots blind to structural disagreement.
- **Decay risk:** As AI and physics models converge (increasingly correlated in 2026), D shrinks and the gate rarely fires — graceful degradation to plain EMOS width, limited upside.
- **Autonomy plan:** Compute D, S per station each cycle; classify regime; pass width multiplier / abstain flag into the H1 PMF builder; size inversely to D via RiskGate; log realized error vs D nightly to keep re-validating.
- **Note:** Build as a feature into H1's width, **not** a separate strategy. If D is not incremental over S (likely correlated), the gate is noise — that is the kill test.

### H4. Variance-regime market selection: trade ladders where ensemble spread is high vs climatology (allocation overlay on H1)
*(merge: H19 "NBM TXNSD variance-regime selection" is the station-product instantiation — same routing logic, TXNSD substituting for ensemble spread.)*
- **Thesis:** Mispricing between a fixed-width crude bot and reality is largest when true uncertainty is far from the assumed constant width. Concentrate the breadth budget on the high-spread tail of each station's spread climatology, where (retail/crude error) is maximized.
- **Signal:** Per station-day, compute spread-percentile = rank of today's pooled-member daily-max std within the station's trailing climatological spread distribution. Trade only the **top tercile** (shoulders/tails mispriced); test but do not assume the bottom tercile. Combine with H1's PMF to pick the mispriced leg.
- **Data:** Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble`; Open-Meteo **Previous-Runs API** (fixed-lead spread climatology, no-lookahead); Kalshi ladders; NWS CLI.
- **Settlement check:** Spread is a selection signal, not a settlement value; traded leg settles on final CLI whole-F, named station, LST. **Spread climatology must be built lead-for-lead from Previous-Runs fixed-lead archives** — mixing leads contaminates the percentile and makes the whole ranking garbage.
- **Backtest + metric:** Rank resolved events by spread-percentile from prior-day data only. Bucket realized market-vs-fair disagreement and realized EV by tercile. Success: top tercile shows materially higher realized EV and larger `|market−fair|` than the middle; Sharpe-per-trade improves when restricting to selected terciles.
- **Capacity:** Filter over the city × day × bucket universe; concentrates the same breadth budget, adds none itself.
- **Beats whom:** Crude fixed-width bots, most wrong on high-spread days.
- **Decay risk:** High-spread dislocation is the most obvious and shrinks first if competitors adopt spread-aware pricing; rotate toward neglected cities flagged stale by H7.
- **Autonomy plan:** Maintain rolling per-station spread climatology; each morning score today's percentile, flag eligible events, allocate by rank across cities; nightly append today's spread.
- **Note:** Fold into H1's sizing — backtest-analysis task, not a separate live strategy. The "bottom-tercile modal underpriced by herd hedging" leg is hand-wavy; test the two terciles separately, expect only the high-spread tail to survive.

### H5. Ensemble-PMF ladder-shape relative value: sell over-concentrated peak, buy underpriced shoulders
- **Thesis:** A point-anchored bot produces a too-peaked implied PMF; because the mutually-exclusive ladder sums to 1, an over-concentrated peak **mechanically** implies underpriced shoulders. Trade the within-event RV pair (sell rich peak, buy cheap shoulders), self-hedging and breadth-scalable — a bet on **market shape error**, not on getting the absolute fair right.
- **Signal:** Renormalize market mids to a PMF; compare its std to the calibrated ensemble PMF std. When `market_std < ensemble_std` by a threshold, short the peak bucket and long the two shoulders in the calibrated mass-difference ratio. Legs net within the `mutually_exclusive` event, reducing capital and fee drag.
- **Data:** Kalshi `/markets?event_ticker=` (all bucket bids/asks one call); Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` (calibrated PMF width); NWS CLI.
- **Settlement check:** Exactly one bucket pays $1; legs settle jointly on the single final CLI whole-F value, named station, LST-windowed. **Re-read floor/cap every event** (geometry shifts daily); never cache. Verify fee per leg via the parabola (cheapest away from 50c).
- **Backtest + metric:** On snapshots.jsonl ladders joined to CLI: for events where `market_std < calibrated ensemble_std`, simulate the sell-peak/buy-shoulders pair to resolution. Metric = positive net-of-fee P&L of the RV pair + improved Brier of calibrated-vs-market PMF shape; confirm edge concentrates on high-spread days. **Calibrate the ensemble PMF first (rank histogram) so the RV fires on market error, not model error.**
- **Capacity:** Every multi-bucket event; net legs free up capital → breadth scales well.
- **Beats whom:** Crude point-anchored bots producing too-peaked ladders; round-number/modal-bucket retail demand.
- **Decay risk:** Sharpening firms flatten the peak themselves → restrict to ladders H7 flags stale. Liquidity to short the rich peak in thin ladders is the real capacity limiter.
- **Autonomy plan:** Pull full ladder per event, build calibrated PMF, compute std gap; when over-concentrated, construct the multi-leg RV maker order, let legs net; RiskGate caps per-event exposure; nightly CLI resolves and updates calibration + ledger.
- **Note:** Best risk-adjusted member of the family. **Fee geometry is asymmetric:** legs near 50c (the peak) cost most (~1.75c) while shoulders are cheaper — model per-leg via the parabola; do not assume the hedge is fee-neutral. Promote alongside H1.

### H6. Ensemble-spread-conditioned tail exceedance vs round-number anchored tail demand
*(merge: this is the canonical tail-fade. H15 "AI-ensemble exceedance tail fade," H24 "climatological tail base-rate fade," H16/H26 "round-number anchor fade" all collapse into this — see notes. It stacks calibration + a behavioral edge.)*
- **Thesis:** Open-ended tails sit at psychologically round thresholds (80/90/100F) attracting anchor-driven retail (CEPR: ~32% taker loss on longshots) and round-number crude-bot quoting. True exceedance is the calibrated, **inflated** ensemble fraction of members beyond the strike. Stacks two independent edges: (a) calibrated exceedance vs crude fixed-width tails, and (b) the favorite-longshot/round-number behavioral bias, which is demand-driven and **does not arb away as easily** as pure calibration.
- **Signal:** For each open-ended tail strike X, compute calibrated exceedance = **inflated** ensemble `P(daily-max beyond X)`. Flag tails where `strike mod 5 == 0`. If `tail_price > inflated_exceedance + fee` → sell the overpriced longshot (fade retail); if `<` → buy. Cross-check vs NBM percentile exceedance for robustness (**optional, not gating** — see note). Maker-only resting orders; tails carry the cheapest fees (~0.6c at P=0.1 vs 1.75c at the money).
- **Data:** Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` per-member daily max (exceedance counts); NBM NBP percentiles (independent cross-check, optional); Kalshi tail markets (T-strikes, floor/cap); NWS CLI; CEPR favorite-longshot prior; station CLI history (climatological exceedance base rate, recent-weighted).
- **Settlement check:** Resolves on final CLI whole-F, named station, LST. **Round-number strikes coincide with integer-C-then-convert quantization breakpoints — the settling lattice is decisive there; discretize.** Cheapest fees live here → tail edge survives fees best.
- **Backtest + metric:** On resolved tails joined to CLI: compare realized hit-rate to (a) market-implied and (b) inflated-ensemble exceedance, **stratified by round vs non-round strikes** (the behavioral kill test). Success: inflated exceedance better-calibrated than market AND round-number tails show a larger market-vs-truth gap; positive maker EV net of tail fee. **Validate the inflation factor on held-out STATIONS, not just held-out dates**, or you overfit the model defect.
- **Capacity:** Two tails per event × all cities × days; small per-leg, cheapest fees, strongly breadth-scalable.
- **Beats whom:** Retail tail/longshot buyers and round-number crude-bot quoting.
- **Decay risk:** If a firm starts arbing ladder tails the anchor distortion closes; H7 gates which cities still carry it.
- **Autonomy plan:** Nightly enumerate open tails, compute calibrated+inflated exceedance (+ optional NBM cross-check), flag round strikes, place maker orders where market deviates beyond fee; size small per longshot economics; rotate across cities; nightly CLI reconciliation adjusts the inflation factor.
- **Notes:** The inflated number is only as good as H1's calibration — **fading a longshot because YOUR model understates the tail is how you blow up on a heat wave.** The forecast-conditioned exceedance must weight the live ensemble heavily near settlement (a forecast-hot day's hot tail is not a longshot). Make NBM cross-check optional (its archive/basis problems break backtesting). Size the rare-hit leg for survival. **Forward-data-collection project: needs ≥80 resolved tails per station; 5-row snapshot gives nothing today.** The `rule_parser.py` currently parses only single `-T{N}` exceedance tickers (`threshold_f=T+1`) — open-ended T-bucket parsing (floor XOR cap) must be built.

## Category B — Intraday Nowcast (settlement-replication; no forecast skill)

Pure arithmetic settlement-statistic replication. These never lose to ECMWF because they use no forecast. H10 is the meta-allocator the entire category routes through; H8 is the core engine; H7/H12/H9 are its phases and correctness properties.

### H7. Running-Max Floor Engine (pre-lock): kill strictly-dominated buckets the instant obs exceed the morning anchor
*(merge: H8 "Post-Peak Lock Sweep" is the limiting case of this engine — see merge note. H27 below is the meta-selector that gates it.)*
- **Thesis:** The running max is a hard monotone lower bound: once observed temp reaches M, the settled high is guaranteed ≥ M, so every bucket strictly below M is worth 0. A point-anchored bot updates only on a new forecast RUN (every 6–12h), so on days where intraday obs climb past the morning forecast, low buckets stay mispriced for hours. Pure one-sided arithmetic; no upside forecast.
- **Signal:** Each minute, compute settling-frame running-max `M_t`. For every bucket with `cap_strike <= M_t`, `fair_prob_yes = 0` → buy NO toward 100c / sell YES toward 0c. Fires all day the moment obs cross a bucket's upper edge, without waiting for the peak. Trigger only when the market still prices that dead bucket > fee+spread.
- **Data:** aviationweather.gov METAR JSON (live running-max, T-group tenths-C); Kalshi `/markets` (floor/cap, book); Kalshi `/series` settlement_sources (station); IEM 1-min ASOS archive (backtest reconstruction only); NWS CLI (settlement truth).
- **Settlement check:** Same CLI/whole-F/LST/2-min-avg basis. **Floor is monotone non-decreasing** → robust to preliminary-vs-final QC (QC trims spurious HIGH spikes, never lowers a sustained running max). Apply the C-rounding guard: only treat a bucket as dead when `M_t` exceeds its cap by **≥1F in the settling frame**, never on a marginal whole-C-rounded reading. **Build the floor from the cleanest sub-degree feed (METAR T-group), not naive max of whole-C 5-min specials** (F→C→F injects ±1–2F phantom error).
- **Backtest + metric:** Reconstruct running-max path per resolved event from IEM 1-min. For each minute and below-floor bucket, check candlestick mid; simulate selling YES / buying NO net of fees. **Metric: hit-rate (~100% by construction — any failure is a settlement-frame reconstruction bug, a powerful self-test); PnL-per-contract after fees; lead-time = minutes between obs-crossing and market re-pricing (the captured staleness window, the right KPI).**
- **Capacity:** Below-floor buckets absorb more size than tail fades (selling toward 0 on impossible buckets); bounded by book depth and adverse selection on thin stale books; size to top-of-book.
- **Beats whom:** Vibe-bot that only steps on a new forecast RUN, leaving a below-floor bucket bid at 5–10c for an extended window; on-venue feed-readers delete this on liquid NYC/LAX within minutes.
- **Decay risk:** Survives in thin secondary-city ladders and the gap between obs-crossing and the next bot forecast tick. **A below-floor bucket bid at 5c is bid by SOMEONE — if that's a firm's resting order you may be adversely selected on a quote you misread.** H10/H27 staleness screen is the required filter to distinguish neglected-book from firm-book.
- **Autonomy plan:** Per-minute METAR loop, active all day: maintain running-max, diff against each open bucket's cap, post marketable-limit where a below-floor bucket is still priced (taker fee acceptable near the cheap 0/100 extremes); log each crossing + reaction lag to feed the staleness ranker; abstain within 1F of a bucket edge.
- **Note:** Best-constructed hypothesis in the set — a monotone arithmetic constraint. **Core engine of the category.**

> **Merged: H8 — Post-Peak Lock Sweep.** The limiting case of H7: after the diurnal peak the running max LOCKS (declare locked when running-max flat ≥90 min, current temp ≥3F below it, and ensemble/HRRR shows no late warm-advection), so below-floor buckets collapse to 0 and the realized bucket → 1. The cited anecdote (`KXHIGHNY-26MAY30-B68.5` at 99/100c post-lock) is **self-incriminating**: if the dead bucket is already 99/100, gross edge ~1c and fees eat it — the most-likely-already-priced of the set. **Live lock detector cannot use hourly METAR T-group as the running-max truth** — CLI settles on the ASOS internal 1-min→5-min running max, which can exceed the last hourly METAR; using METAR as the floor understates it and can mislabel a still-alive bucket as dead near a 1F edge. Keep only as the post-peak phase of H7, capacity-limited, routed through the staleness screen — not standalone alpha.

### H9. Settlement-frame replicator: trade the C-round-then-convert quantization crude bots ignore
- **Thesis:** The official high = round(5-min running avg to whole °C) then convert to F (or the ASOS internal whole-F max). Near a bucket boundary the true settling distribution is **lumpy on a coarse Celsius grid** (20C=68F, 21C=70F), and the F↔C round-trip can flip 78F→79F. We replicate the exact quantization, so on edge-adjacent buckets the market prices as continuous/certain we have a calibrated probabilistic edge AND avoid self-inflicted basis loss. **The most defensible processing edge in the catalog — not a "read obs faster" play (firms win that) but a "model the discrete settlement grid correctly" play crude continuous-logistic bots structurally cannot do.**
- **Signal:** When the live running-max sits within ~1.5F of a 2F bucket boundary, build the discrete settlement-value distribution: model the underlying analog temperature, apply round-to-whole-C-then-F (and the alternative whole-F internal-max path), producing a PMF over the few admissible settling F values straddling the edge. Fade buckets the market prices as near-certain (>90c/<10c) when the quantization PMF assigns materially more mass to the neighbor.
- **Data:** aviationweather.gov METAR T-group (tenths-C); IEM 1-min/5-min ASOS archive (reconstruct the quantization chain); ASOS User's Guide v2.80 algorithm appendix (authoritative sampling/averaging/rounding spec); Kalshi `/markets`; NWS CLI (calibrate the quantization model).
- **Settlement check:** This hypothesis IS a settlement-basis model. **Validate the assumed chain (whole-C round vs whole-F internal max) against historical CLI per station** — stations may differ. **Pre-compute per-station which bucket boundaries are C-breakpoint-aligned (quantization-sensitive) vs "safe," and only arm on quantization-sensitive days.** LST windowing; preliminary-vs-final apply.
- **Backtest + metric:** Per resolved station-day, run the late-afternoon analog trace through the candidate chain, compare predicted settled F to actual CLI to fit/validate the rounding model (no-lookahead rolling fit). On held-out edge-adjacent days, compare quantization-PMF edge-bucket prob to market mid; simulate fading overconfident edge buckets. Metric: Brier/log-loss of the quantization PMF beating a continuous-logistic baseline; PnL on faded positions.
- **Capacity:** Likely thin — a **precision sniper, not a volume engine**; depends on enough days landing with the running-max within ~1.5F of a quantization-sensitive edge.
- **Beats whom:** Logistic-anchored bots that price the edge bucket as smoothly near-certain.
- **Decay risk:** Least likely to be deleted of the whole set (structural processing, not latency). Edge is only as good as the analog-temperature model feeding the quantizer — but the running-max floor anchors the location, bounding self-inflicted basis error.
- **Autonomy plan:** Maintain a per-station fitted quantization model (refit nightly from IEM+CLI). Intraday, when running-max enters a bucket's edge zone, compute the discrete PMF, check the market price, post maker orders fading overconfidence where edge>fee+spread (targets exactly the cheap >90c/<10c quotes).

### H12. Pre-peak diurnal climb model: state-dependent residual-warming distribution
- **Thesis:** Before the peak the high is not locked but the remaining uncertainty has an estimable shape: residual climb from the current running-max, perturbed by cloud timing and ensemble spread. By midday the floor has eaten the downside, so the true distribution is **one-sided narrowing** — exactly what a fixed symmetric band gets wrong.
- **Signal:** `fair_high_distribution = running-max_floor` convolved with a residual-warming term `= f(hours to peak, observed morning warming rate, ensemble spread of remaining-hours temp, cloud forecast)`. As the day progresses, mean rises toward the floor and variance collapses. Map to bucket probs, renormalize, trade where the market PMF (still ~symmetric around morning forecast) disagrees.
- **Data:** aviationweather.gov METAR (morning warming rate + running-max); Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` (remaining-hours spread); Open-Meteo `/v1/forecast` `minutely_15` + cloudcover; Open-Meteo Historical-Forecast/Previous-Runs (no-lookahead features); Kalshi `/markets`; NWS CLI.
- **Settlement check:** Floor from station obs in the settling frame; residual-warming model is a FEATURE not settlement. Ensemble/minutely is a grid cell → per-station bias offset (floor anchors location, so bias matters less). LST window for floor and remaining-hours. Whole-F integration; abstain near edges.
- **Backtest + metric:** Replay: at each pre-peak minute, build the conditional distribution from (IEM running-max as-of-t) + (Previous-Runs ensemble as-of); compare to candlestick PMF and CLI. Metric: CRPS/log-loss vs the fixed-2.5F baseline **sliced by time-of-day (must show increasing skill as the floor tightens)**; PnL of disagreement trades. **Kill test: if the edge comes from the MEAN term rather than the SHAPE term, kill it — the mean term is forecasting and loses to NBM/ensemble in the price.**
- **Capacity:** Few buckets per event across temp ladders; conditional, lower priority than H1/H5.
- **Beats whom:** Genuinely crude/symmetric bot quotes in neglected ladders, in the midday-to-pre-peak window.
- **Decay risk:** The moment your residual term is a point estimate of climb, you are forecasting and lose. Mitigation: anchor LOCATION entirely on the obs floor; let the model contribute ONLY variance-collapse/one-sidedness, fitted as a spread not a mean.
- **Autonomy plan:** Fit residual-warming spread per station/season nightly from IEM; intraday update the conditional distribution each minute from live obs + morning ensemble, price the ladder, trade disagreements as maker. The lock engine (H8) is its limiting case (variance→0 after peak).
- **Note:** Benchmark head-to-head against simply using NBM/ensemble percentiles directly (H17) which may dominate with less overfit risk.

### H13. Low-temp morning lock: settle the daily LOW once post-dawn warming begins
- **Thesis:** Symmetric to the high lock but on the OTHER side of the day and a thinner, more-neglected family (`KXLOW*`, fewer cities, often absent in summer). The daily LOW is a running MIN set near dawn; once temps climb steadily it is a one-sided locking ceiling. KXLOW books are thinner/less firm-attended → staleness is larger.
- **Signal:** Track running-MIN in the settling frame through early-morning LST. Declare LOCKED when temp has risen ≥3F above the running-min for ≥60 min, post-dawn warming established, AND ensemble shows no late-night/pre-dawn cold front for the **remainder of the LST day**. ### H10. Price-vs-feed responsiveness screen: rank ladders by how fast they react to boundary crossings (the meta-edge)
*(merge: H27 "Feed-Responsiveness Staleness Rank" and H32 "responsiveness ranker as cross-market allocation layer" are the same meta-edge stated three times — fully merged here. This is the single most important item in the catalog.)*
- **Thesis:** EV survives only where the market is STALE relative to the obs feed. Staleness score = weighted combo; rank all weather markets, allocate to top-staleness, drop sharpening ones.
- **Data:** Kalshi candlesticks `/series/{s}/markets/{t}/candlesticks?period_interval=1` (+ Batch Get Candlesticks) — **yes_bid/yes_ask OHLC confirmed available, persists on zero-volume minutes**; Kalshi orderbook (spread/depth/quote-update freq); aviationweather.gov METAR + IEM 1-min archive (feed-implied-fair path); NWS CLI.
- **Settlement check:** The feed-implied-fair path **MUST be built in the correct settling frame (LST, whole-F, 2-min-avg, ASOS internal running max)** — otherwise crossings are mis-timed and a SHARP market gets mislabeled STALE, routing capital INTO a firm = max adverse selection. Same replicator as H7/H9, reused as a diagnostic. No direct settlement exposure (it selects markets).
- **Backtest + metric:** On resolved-day candlesticks joined to reconstructed obs paths, compute staleness metrics, then test whether high-staleness markets delivered higher realized edge for the floor/lock engines than low-staleness. **Metric: rank-correlation between prior-week staleness and next-period realized edge (Spearman>0.4 for forward usability); confirmation that abstaining on low-staleness cuts adverse-selection losses.** Normalize reaction by feed-move size; exclude post-lock periods (flat = certainty, not neglect).
- **Capacity:** Adds none itself; concentrates the fixed breadth budget into the ~10–20 stalest city/bucket/day combos.
- **Beats whom:** Routes us away from the firm-owned book and into the crude-bot-set book; the allocation brain for the whole category.
- **Decay risk:** This IS the rotation engine — edge is decaying rent by design. Risk is the screen lagging real-time sharpening within a single day, and **measurement error** (mis-replicated feed → mislabeled staleness).
- **Autonomy plan:** Nightly pull prior-N-days 1-min candlesticks for every weather market (Batch endpoint), reconstruct obs-fair paths from IEM, compute staleness scores, write a ranked allowlist. Intraday engines (H7/H8/H9/H12/H13) consult the allowlist and only trade top-ranked markets, sizing to depth. A rolling "effective edge captured" per series triggers auto-rotation out.
- **Notes (data reality):** **Backtest ground-truth is fragile.** IEM `asos1min` returned header-only (0 rows) for NYC/LGA/ORD/MDW in testing, and the 5-min ASOS archive returns "Entries Found -1" for KNYC (Central Park) and `tmpf=M` for LGA on recent dates — IEM lags realtime by days-to-weeks and KNYC is co-op-flaky. **Workable substitute:** reconstruct the fair path from hourly+SPECI METAR T-groups (aviationweather.gov works, KNYC emits clean tenths-C T-groups), which is the settlement-frame floor anyway — but cadence is hourly-to-~20-min, NOT 1-min, so redefine staleness on **obs-event windows** (post-SPECI ~20-min reaction) rather than literal minutes. Fix datasource field names: read `volume_fp`/`open_interest_fp`/`yes_bid_dollars`/`yes_ask_dollars`. **Build this FIRST — it is the prerequisite that makes H1/H5/H6/H7 real rather than guesses about neglect.**

### H11. DST/LST window-flip correctness property

## Category C — Tail & Structural (model-free / behavioral)

### H14. Tail-CDF monotonicity / shape-violation locks
- **Thesis:** Across open-ended tails the implied survival function `P(high≥X)` must be non-increasing in X, and the ladder PMF must be single-peaked. Stale quotes, round-number anchoring, and thin one-sided books create logically-impossible local inversions and bimodal artifacts — **model-free relative-value locks** tradeable for guaranteed non-negative payoff regardless of realized temperature.
- **Signal:** Order the ladder by strike. Check survival monotonicity across above-X strikes and PMF unimodality across interior buckets. Flag any pair where the higher-threshold (less-likely) claim trades richer than the lower-threshold claim by more than spread+fee. Trade the dominated pair: buy underpriced more-likely leg, sell overpriced less-likely leg.
- **Data:** Kalshi `/markets?event_ticker=` (all bid/ask, floor/cap); Kalshi orderbook (executable depth); Kalshi candlesticks (confirm inversion persists, not a momentary crossed quote); snapshots.jsonl.
- **Settlement check:** Monotonicity is exact **only on the integer-F C-derived settling grid** — because settlement rounds whole-C then converts, the integer highs each strike covers can be NON-contiguous, so two F-adjacent strikes may have a gap/overlap on the true settling set. **Compute nesting on the C-derived F set** or you flag phantom inversions that are correct pricing of the rounding gap. Test on bids/asks you can actually hit (wide overnight spreads create phantom inversions in mids). Inversion must exceed two-leg fee. **Verify the dominance arithmetic per-pair** (the third state where neither pays means payoff is bounded but not always strictly non-negative).
- **Backtest + metric:** Scan historical snapshots/candlesticks for violations in resolved ladders; for each flagged pair simulate the trade at executable bid/ask, settle on CLI. Metric: fraction of flags real (executable) vs phantom (spread artifacts); realized locked payoff net of fees; correlation of violation frequency with the staleness score.
- **Capacity:** Opportunistic, infrequent, small per-pair depth — a monitoring overlay, not an income stream.
- **Beats whom:** Crude bots setting each strike independently; no one polices cross-strike monotonicity in neglected ladders.
- **Decay risk:** Genuine executable violations are rare, fleeting, and arrive-after-gone given public-feed latency; concentrated in the most neglected small-city overnight books.
- **Autonomy plan:** WS orderbook listener per event computes the survival curve/PMF on each delta; when a violation persists across N≥2 snapshots and clears fees, fire the locking pair as marketable-limit sized to min leg depth; RiskGate confirms same-station settlement; nightly false-positive audit.
- **Note:** Keep at low capacity budget.

- **Capacity:** One window per event/day (~13 series), thin morning/overnight depth; wide-spread abstention cuts most candidates → a few fills/day. Breadth across cities is the scaler.

- **Decay risk:** Requires a competitor to be awake re-seeding at open — rare in firm-ignored small cities; the carried gap closes in minutes if anyone is. Decays if Kalshi changes listing cadence.
- **Autonomy plan:** Scheduler fires at each series' open_time and the first post-13Z-ensemble tick; fetch seeded ladder + freshest ensemble; measure gap, idleness, spread; if gap>threshold, idle, spread reasonable, ensemble newer than last quote, place maker orders sized to thin-book depth; cancel trigger on a 3c mid move or 13Z NBM arrival.

- **Settlement check:** Truth = final CLI whole-F for the EXACT `issuedby` station (CHI=KMDW not KORD — repo bug). **Residual MUST be computed against CLI, never ERA5/Open-Meteo civil-day max (that bakes in the bias being measured).** Re-window forecast hourly temps to LST before taking the max. Abstain within ~1.5F of a bucket edge.
- **Backtest + metric:** Replay resolved events; fit rolling_bias on prior station-days only. Metric: Brier/log-loss of bias-corrected ladder vs the raw-forecast logistic baseline AND PnL net of fees on the max-disagreement leg with a reliability-diagram check. Edge confirmed if Brier improves AND PnL CLV-positive on held-out months.
- **Capacity:** 8+ HIGH cities × ~6 buckets × daily; scales by breadth, not size.
- **Beats whom:** Crude bots and retail trusting the raw grid forecast.
- **Decay risk:** The single most obvious correction — a firm in a city ladder learns the station-minus-grid offset trivially. **Gate every trade on H10: "mid has NOT moved toward the de-biased value."** Without that gate this is EV-deleted in liquid cities. Bias drifts seasonally → rolling window absorbs it.
- **Autonomy plan:** Nightly: pull each station's prior-day CLI (IEM CF6), append to a residual log, recompute rolling_bias; at trade time pull live forecast + ladder, apply bias, renormalize, emit `fair_prob`; decide() compares with fee buffer; RiskGate sizes; log fills/CLV for the decay monitor.
- **Note:** `weather.py` uses `temperature_2m_max` = civil-day max, so the measured "bias" silently absorbs the LST-windowing error (H11) — **decompose them or you misattribute.** Build the IEM truth-pipeline first or the backtest is vacuous.

### H38. Conditional (wind/sky/season) station-minus-grid bias regression for overnight LOW markets (UHI)
- **Thesis:** Urban-heat-island physics makes the station-minus-grid residual **state-dependent**: the urban-rural delta is largest on clear+calm nights and in winter, suppressed by wind/cloud/precip. A crude bot applies (at best) a constant bias. KXLOW is thinner/more seasonal/more neglected, so the conditional structure survives. **Higher surviving edge than H20** — a mean-offset feed-reader is still systematically wrong on calm-clear nights.
- **Signal:** Fit `residual_low = a + b·windspeed_10m + c·cloudcover + d·season_dummy + e·onshore_flag` per station on trailing history; predict today's conditional bias from today's forecast wind/sky and shift the LOW distribution. Trigger on calm+clear+winter nights where the model predicts the grid is too warm and the market hasn't priced it.
- **Data:** Open-Meteo live + historical forecast: `temperature_2m_min`, `windspeed_10m`, `cloudcover`, `winddirection_10m` (one call); NWS CLI daily MIN (per-market `rules_primary` station); Kalshi `KXLOW` ladders (seasonal).
- **Settlement check:** Final CLI whole-F daily MIN; **station from per-market `rules_primary`** (series-level URL is generic). **LST re-windowing matters MORE for lows** (the min often occurs near dawn / the LST boundary). Coastal stations (LAX/MIA/NYC) need the onshore/offshore flag from `winddirection_10m`. Abstain within ~1.5F of a bucket edge.
- **Backtest + metric:** Assemble (forecast wind/sky/season, CLI_low) tuples per station via Previous-Runs; fit the regression on a training split, predict on held-out station-days. Metric: OOS residual-prediction R² vs a constant-bias baseline; then ladder Brier vs fixed logistic; then net-fee PnL on calm-clear triggers. Confirmed if conditional model beats constant-bias on held-out R² AND ladder Brier.
- **Capacity:** 5 LOW cities × ~6 buckets, seasonal (winter strongest); moderate breadth, small size.
- **Beats whom:** Crude bots using a constant/zero station bias AND mean-offset feed-readers (the conditional structure is a higher bar to replicate).
- **Decay risk:** UHI weak/absent in summer (abstain); coefficients drift → weekly refit. Thin LOW books → adverse selection; size to displayed depth.
- **Autonomy plan:** Weekly refit per station from the CLI residual log; at trade time the single Open-Meteo call computes the conditional bias, shifts the LOW CDF, integrates, feeds decide(); trade only when predicted `|bias|` exceeds threshold and H10 flags the market stale.
- **Note:** Most defensible thesis in the bias family — the edge is a processing **structure** (conditional bias) both crude AND mean-offset price-setters miss. Risk: with <1 winter of replayable data you can overfit 4+ coefficients on a few hundred nights; demand min-N per regime.

### H28. Multi-product μ-divergence as a regime-uncertainty / abstain-or-widen gate
*(merge: this is the climatology-family statement of H3/H21; the distinguishing feature is the three concrete public products and the explicit "replace the fixed logistic with ensemble spread TODAY" interim path.)*
- **Thesis:** When independent central-tendency sources disagree, genuine uncertainty is elevated beyond any single product's spread. Compute μ from three free sources (Open-Meteo ensemble median, GFS MOS MAV/MEX, NBM NBP P50) and use their disagreement as a state-dependent width multiplier.
- **Signal:** `mu_spread = stdev(OpenMeteo_median, MOS_NX, NBP_P50)`. Tight agreement (< low quartile) AND market center < calibrated fair → buy the center. Wide disagreement (> high quartile) → fatten tails / abstain. Blend the three μ into a location, use mu_spread to inflate width.
- **Data:** Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` (median); GFS MOS MAV/MEX text via NOMADS (`mdl_gfsmav`/`mex`); NBM NBP text via NOMADS (`TXNP5` = P50, self-snapshot — no archive); Kalshi ladder.
- **Settlement check:** All three are FEATURES at station ICAO; none is the settling statistic (CLI whole-F). NBP Max window (12Z–06Z) ≠ CLI LST day — use P50 as location only, never NBP width raw (NBM under-dispersed). MOS has no spread (μ only). Map each product's station to the exact CLI `issuedby` (KMDW for CHI). Abstain on missing NBP rows (full 01/07/13/19Z cycles only).
- **Backtest + metric:** **Forward-snapshot NBM/MOS into snapshots.jsonl (no archive).** Once logged: does mu_spread predict realized `|error|`? (positive correlation = the kill test, run first). Does width-conditioning improve event log-loss vs unconditioned? PnL of buy-center-on-agreement net fees.
- **Capacity:** Selectivity/sizing overlay on H1 — raises EV-per-trade, adds no legs.
- **Beats whom:** Single-point-forecast crude bots blind to cross-model agreement.
- **Decay risk:** Low — independent public products will always occasionally disagree (structural meta-signal). But requires the NBM snapshot corpus before any backtest is valid.
- **Autonomy plan:** Cron snapshots NBP (13Z full) + MOS daily into snapshots.jsonl; at trade time parse the three μ by row LABEL (version-robust), compute mu_spread vs climatology, set width multiplier, integrate, trade center-on-agreement / abstain-on-disagreement.
- **Notes:** **Not three independent calibrations** — NBP is a blend that ingests GFS (→ MOS/MAV), and Open-Meteo leans on GFS/ECMWF; D is a correlated-residual signal, so thresholds (0.5·TXNSD / TXNSD) need empirical calibration, not assumed. **Data-access:** `tgftp.nws.noaa.gov` MOS/LAMP returned 403 in this environment; obtain MOS via NOMADS, not tgftp. **Cheap interim version: replace the fixed-2.5F logistic with Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` spread TODAY** (Historical-Forecast has ensemble, no archive problem); add MOS/NBP divergence once snapshotted.

> **Merged: H17/H22 — NBP percentile fan → calibrated bucket PMF.** The station-product instantiation of H1. **Verified live:** `blend_nbptx.tCCz` exists on NOMADS (keyless, header "NBM V5.0 NBP GUIDANCE"); KNYC carries `TXNP1/2/5/7/9` + `TXNSD`. ## Category F — Cross-Market & Portfolio Risk

### H39. Multi-city heat-dome correlation cap: prevent breadth-stacking from secretly concentrating one synoptic bet
*(merge: H40 "Cross-City Airmass Correlation Cap" is the same rule stated twice — fully merged.)*
- **Thesis:** A breadth strategy that stacks many city buckets can silently become one concentrated synoptic bet on regional heat-dome/cold-snap days, when 1–3 day Tmax forecast errors become highly common-mode. Naive breadth overstates diversification; without a cap a single synoptic miss can wipe a quarter's edge. For a one-person operator, survival/bankroll is the binding constraint — this protects the ability to keep harvesting H1/H6/H7.
- **Signal:** Each day, compute pairwise ensemble-error correlation / common-mode loading across held cities (PCA on per-member city-high anomalies, or a shared 500mb-ridge signal). When the common-mode factor exceeds a threshold (cross-city daily-max member correlation > ~0.6), cap aggregate same-airmass notional and haircut new correlated positions; independent days allow full breadth. Output = per-market size multiplier.
- **Data:** Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` **multi-city single call** (comma-separated lat/lon — verified keyless/available); Open-Meteo Previous-Runs (fixed-lead error-correlation history); CPC ONI + daily AO/NAO/PNA (slow regime stratifier); internal position book; NWS CLI multi-city history (backtest covariance).
- **Settlement check:** No new settlement basis — each city leg settles independently on its own station CLI. The rule operates on already-correctly-specified legs; it does NOT create cross-station equivalence claims (which would be the basis trap). Correlation is in forecast errors, not settlement statistics.
- **Backtest + metric:** Reconstruct as-of cross-city ensemble correlation per day (no-lookahead); simulate naive equal-breadth vs correlation-capped portfolios over the resolved-market panel. Metric: capped portfolio achieves higher Sharpe / lower max-drawdown for equal-or-better total post-fee PnL; on tagged heat-dome days the capped loss tail is materially smaller.
- **Capacity:** Reduces gross on correlated days deliberately; does not bind on independent days. Net effect: steadier capital utilization.
- **Beats whom:** Not a counterparty edge — a self-discipline rule preventing the breadth book from becoming an accidental single bet.
- **Decay risk:** Very low — structural risk rule, not a market-inefficiency bet.
- **Autonomy plan:** After per-city engines select legs, compute the common-mode factor on the pooled ensemble, derive per-leg size haircuts, pass to RiskGate before orders go out; log the factor; tighten caps automatically on flagged dome days.
- **Note:** Estimate correlation from same-lead member spread across cities (state-dependent), not climatology, so the cap doesn't bind on flat-gradient days. With only ~6–8 cities the correlation matrix is low-dimensional and the dome sample small → calibrate the threshold conservatively rather than over-fit "dome days." Must compose with adjacent-day exposure so a multi-day ridge isn't double-counted. **Plumbing into RiskGate, not a research priority.**

## Category G — Market-Selection Meta (the rotation controller)

### H27b. Post-Peak Lock Convergence-Gap Selector
*(This is the selector view of H8/H10 applied to the lock: it ranks markets by the gap between feed-implied certainty and price-implied certainty. Kept distinct from H8 because the verified market-structure runway makes it the strongest survivor.)*
- **Thesis:** After the diurnal peak the high is monotonically locked, so the settling answer is near-deterministic hours before CLI issues. A sharp book snaps the winning bucket to ~99c within minutes; a crude book lingers near its morning probability. The 2–4h pre-settlement gap requires **zero forecasting skill — only correct floor arithmetic** — so it survives the "you lose to ECMWF" constraint.
- **Signal:** In the post-peak window (running max flat ≥90 min AND HRRR/ensemble shows no late warm front): `feed_certainty = 1.0` for the locked bucket, 0 for buckets strictly below; `price_certainty` = market mid. `convergence_gap = feed_certainty − price_certainty`. Select markets where gap > edge+fee and the running-max replication is **≥1.5F clear of the nearest bucket edge** (dodge the C-round flip). Trade the locked bucket / sell the dead buckets.
- **Data:** aviationweather.gov METAR T-group (live running max); Open-Meteo `/v1/forecast` `minutely_15` + HRRR (late-warming guard); Kalshi `/markets/{t}/orderbook` (executable price); NWS CLI (labels).
- **Settlement check:** Running max = CLI-frame (2-min-avg, whole-F, LST), built from hourly+SPECI METAR T-group (tenths-C, cleanest), NOT naive max of 5-min whole-C specials. **Final CLI can trim a preliminary high by 1F on QC** (confirmed in `rules_secondary`) → the **≥1.5F-inside rule is mandatory**, never trade a bucket whose edge is within conversion noise of the running max. Wrong station = disqualifying (KMDW not KORD).
- **Backtest + metric:** Reconstruct the running-max path per resolved market-day from hourly/SPECI METAR; identify lock time (flat ≥90 min); pull candlestick mid at lock+15/+60/+120 min; compute gap + realized payoff (won/lost per CLI). No-lookahead. **Metric: among locked markets, realized win-rate ≥90% AND average gap captured net of fees > 4c; false-lock rate (late warm front broke the lock) < 2%.** Fee check: at P=0.92, maker = taker = ~$0.01/contract, so the >4c threshold clears comfortably.
- **Capacity:** Resting size on dead/locked buckets in stale books (often the only maker is us). **Verified runway:** markets trade until 11:59pm ET (close_time 04:59Z) while the high locks early-mid afternoon → 6–9 hours of post-lock tradability. Scales by cities × clean-lock days (most clear-sky summer days qualify).
- **Beats whom:** Crude bot still anchored to its morning point forecast hours after the high locked; retail holding residual-upside buckets. The **dead buckets** (selling residual upside the running-max has provably exceeded) are where retail tail-buyers and crude bots leave money and where the firm cares least.
- **Decay risk:** Medium — the most conceptually obvious edge, most likely to attract a simple feed-reader. Gated by H10/H42 so it self-rotates out of cities that acquire a fast book.
- **Autonomy plan:** Intraday loop polls METAR ~every 10 min per selected city; lock-detector flags flat-and-past-peak; reads live orderbook, checks the C-round margin + no-late-warming guard, emits marketable-limit on the locked bucket; auto-flattens at settlement; runs only on cities passing the staleness selector.
- **Note:** Strongest survivor — testable end-to-end (needs only the daily running-max path from hourly/SPECI METAR + CLI final as label, not a 1-min series), defensible surviving edge in dead buckets of thin cities.

### H42. Spread-Compression Rotate-Out Trigger (sharpening detector — the exit half)
- **Thesis:** Edge is decaying rent. The earliest observable symptom of a market sharpening (a feed-reader arriving) is **time-weighted bid-ask compression + rising quote-update frequency — before realized fill edge visibly decays.** Detecting it early lets us rotate out one step ahead rather than via losses. The disciplined exit half of the meta-edge most bots lack.
- **Signal:** Per series, week-over-week track: time-weighted spread (cents), distinct-quote-count/hour, depth refill speed after a trade, price-to-feed beta (toward 1.0). Composite `sharpening_score`; flag rotate-out when spread compresses below a floor (e.g. <2c sustained) OR beta crosses ~0.7 OR rolling effective-edge-captured drops below threshold; reallocate the freed budget to higher-staleness series.
- **Data:** Kalshi candlesticks (`yes_bid`/`yes_ask` OHLC, `volume_fp`, `open_interest_fp` — verified per-1-min); Kalshi orderbook depth snapshots; internal fill log.
- **Settlement check:** Microstructure screen, no direct basis risk. The price-to-feed-beta input inherits H10's fair-path dependency (and its IEM-lag fragility); spread/quote-count/depth-refill need NO fair path → compute the trigger **primarily from venue-internal metrics, beta secondary.**
- **Backtest + metric:** Compute weekly microstructure metrics per series; correlate with the FOLLOWING week's realized floor-snipe edge. Metric: `sharpening_score` has **negative predictive correlation** with next-week realized edge; a rotate-out rule beats static allocation on cumulative post-fee PnL across the panel.
- **Capacity:** Meta-layer; reallocates the fixed breadth budget, improves capital efficiency.
- **Beats whom:** Ourselves-in-the-past (avoids feeding a freshly-arrived firm); indirectly the firm by exiting before it prices us out.
- **Decay risk:** Low — microstructure sharpening signals are robust and venue-structural; the rule itself doesn't decay, only the markets it points at.
- **Autonomy plan:** Weekly cron computes per-series microstructure trend, updates the allocation table the strategy reads; series crossing the threshold are removed automatically; freed budget shifts to the staleness-rank top.
- **Note:** Cleanest single signal = your OWN rolling effective-edge-captured trending down (you have a fill log) combined with sustained spread<2c — self-contained and lag-free. **H10 + H42 is the actual product.**

### H41. Neglect-by-Volume × Variance Pre-Screen (cold-start market picker)
- **Thesis:** Before any price history exists, two cheap public priors predict neglect: (1) low series volume/open-interest (weather is ~0.2% of Kalshi; within it MIA/DEN/AUS and deep-OTM tail buckets are thinnest), and (2) high climatological forecast variance (DEN's terrain-driven swings make a fixed-width anchor especially wrong). The product `(low volume) × (high spread-of-outcome)` maximizes firm-disinterest and crude-bot-error simultaneously — a sensible cold-start allocation before H10 has data.
- **Signal:** `neglect_prior = z(low open_interest_fp + low volume_fp) × z(ensemble-spread-percentile-of-climatology)`. Rank; seed the breadth portfolio toward high-prior series and off-modal/tail buckets. Hand off to H10 as soon as candlestick history accrues.
- **Data:** Kalshi `/markets` `open_interest_fp`, `volume_fp` per series; Open-Meteo `/v1/ensemble` (daily-max member spread); Open-Meteo Historical-Forecast (climatological spread baseline); NWS CLI.
- **Settlement check:** Variance input is a forecast feature (grid ensemble fine here); the eventual trade settles on station CLI → **nominate only clean NWS-CLI series; exclude `KXRAINMIA` (AccuWeather) and `KXRAINNY` (USGS gauge 01376520)** — confirmed non-NWS in the reference doc.
- **Backtest + metric:** Build `neglect_prior` from as-of data (volume/OI snapshot + as-of ensemble spread); correlate with realized post-fee edge of a vanilla calibrated-PMF strategy in that series-day. Metric: top-quartile prior shows >3c higher realized edge than bottom quartile; **prior ranking agrees with the eventual H10 responsiveness rank at Spearman > 0.3** (validates the cold-start proxy AND is the honest kill-switch).
- **Capacity:** Directs breadth toward the structurally-thinnest markets — smallest per-market size, broadest count.
- **Beats whom:** Retail tail-buying in thin deep-OTM buckets; crude bots whose fixed width is most wrong in high-variance cities like DEN.
- **Decay risk:** Low-medium; volume/variance structure is slow-moving. **Risk: high variance can flag the most-CONTESTED market (firms chase variance=opportunity), not the most-neglected** — survives only where low-volume AND high-variance coincide AND H10/H3 confirm no fast book.
- **Autonomy plan:** Daily cron pulls volume/OI + ensemble spread, computes the prior, emits an initial weighting used only for series lacking candlestick history; H10 overrides once available; new listings auto-enter via the prior.
- **Note:** A funnel input, never a trade trigger — use ONLY to order the queue for H10; require H10 confirmation before any capital.

> **Merged: H43 — Time-of-Day Liquidity-Window Scheduler.** An orchestration wrapper, not alpha: it maps the intraday clock to (which edge, maker/taker, size) — W1 open = H18, W2 post-13Z = H1, W3 pre-peak = H12, W4 post-peak = H27b/H8. ## Caveats & Kill-Criteria

**What would falsify the whole approach:**
- **The neglect term goes to zero.** The entire catalog rests on `firm-disinterest > 0`. Verified market structure already shows some "neglected" books are not: `KXHIGHNY-26MAY31` had OI=1386 and `yes_bid_size=861` — a real maker, not a stale crude bot. If H10 consistently ranks the *liquid* universe as already-sharp and the *thin* surviving universe is too small to fill, the strategy cannot reach ~$50k/yr. **Kill criterion:** if, after a full forward-collection quarter, the top-staleness decile cannot absorb enough breadth (top-of-book depth × fillable markets) to clear costs, the apex-bot-in-neglected-markets thesis is falsified for this venue.
- **We are the stale party.** Every "fade the lagging book" hypothesis (H7/H8/H18/H27b) assumes the resting counterparty is a crude bot, not a firm with a fresher read. If realized fills are systematically adversely selected (we get filled right before the floor breaks, or right before a fresh run moves the price our way), the edge is inverted. **Kill:** negative realized CLV on H10-gated trades.
- **Backtest cannot exist yet.** `snapshots.jsonl` has ~5 rows; Kalshi archives no orderbook depth; NBM/MOS have no archive; the AIFS ensemble archive is ~3 months; IEM 1-min ASOS is lag- and gap-ridden (KNYC flaky). **Several hypotheses (H6, H17/H22, H28, H29, H35) are forward-data-collection projects** — they cannot be validated before going live, only after months of self-snapshotting. Do not size them until the corpus exists.

**Capacity ceilings (the binding constraint):**
- Per-market top-of-book depth is tiny (tens to low-hundreds of contracts; many tail/thin books <$50–200). The whole book is "a few hundred dollars notional/day across cities." This is a **breadth play, not a size play** — total throughput is the product of (number of demonstrably-stale markets) × (small per-market depth). H39 deliberately *reduces* capacity on correlated heat-dome days. The realistic ceiling, not the per-trade edge, determines whether ~$50k/yr is reachable.
